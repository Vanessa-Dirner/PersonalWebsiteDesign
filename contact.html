FAQ
How did you end up in both fine arts and IT? I was interested in both from a young age. You could find me drawing, dancing, singing as well as building, experimenting with and destroying computers.
I see some of the art says reproduction. What does that mean? As with any field, learning by copying masters is the best way to advance skills. This stretches the artist's mind to think in the way of someone else.
Are the graphic design examples for real company's? Yes, all of the graphic design was for real clients through my workplaces.
Tell me about the Project Plan? It was a collaborative work created for a scenario, which we presented as a powerpoint to our audience.
Do you have any other examples? Yes, I plan to put up more as time goes on, but if you have something specific you would like to see let me know.
How did you learn all these skills? While I did learn a fair amount through school, I find on the job learning to be the most efficient way, along with experimenting on my own time.
What do you mean by experimenting? I have my own home lab consisting of a server, multiple switches and more that I use to learn. I like to dabble in virtualization and legacy software.
How do you learn all of this? I'm an avid reader, with an entire bookshelf on various topics in business and finance, operating systems like DOS, Oracle, Unix, Red Hat Linux and programming like Visual C, C, C++ Java, and Pl/SQL.
